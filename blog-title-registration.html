Registering Your Title Deed in Kenya

Updated: January 28, 2026 Surv. Martin Kimani 9 Min Read

Quick Answer

Land registration in Kenya is now primarily digital via ArdhiSasa. The process involves four key documents: 1. Transfer form; 2. Land rent clearance; 3. Stamp duty valuation; and 4. The final Mutation/Survey plan. Total processing time typically ranges from 21 to 60 days depending on registry speed.

In This Article:
  • Essential Documents for Registration
  • Navigating the ArdhiSasa Portal
  • Stamp Duty and Valuation Process
  • Final Issuance of the Title

The issuance of a Title Deed is the final, most critical step in the land acquisition process. It provides absolute proof of ownership and is the only document recognized by banks for financing.

1. Essential Documents for Registration

  • Transfer Form: Duly executed by both buyer and seller.
  • Original Title: The mother title must be surrendered for subdivision or transfer.
  • KRA PIN & ID: Copies for all parties involved.
  • Passport Photos: Usually two for each party.
  • Land Rates Clearance: From the local County Government.

2. Navigating the ArdhiSasa Portal

With the digital shift, both parties must have verified profiles on ArdhiSasa. The seller initiates the transfer, and the buyer "accepts" it digitally. This system has virtually eliminated the "disappearing files" problem of the manual era.

3. Stamp Duty and Valuation Process

The government must value the land to determine the Stamp Duty (usually 2% for rural land and 4% for urban land). A government valuer from the Ministry of Lands will visit the site before the duty is assessed.

Ensure your beacons are visible for the valuer; see our Beacon Verification Guide for help.

4. Final Issuance of the Title

Once duty is paid and the registry processes the file, a new Title Deed (or Certificate of Lease) is issued in the buyer's name. Always perform a post-registration search to confirm the data has been accurately updated.
